TehManticore~Eliri~L12 31-May-21 08:30 PM
Didn't know where else to put this: When you've got something like Bloodwell Vial or Rod of the Pact Keeper that increases your attack/spell DC, but you're multiclassed so you don't want it to increase all attacks/DCs, you can specify it a little bit. When you choose "Spellcasting Ability" for a spell, you can choose any of the stats or the "Spell" stat. Spell stat is the one named at the top of your spell tab under "Spellcasting Ability". When you have something like the vial or rod that increases attack or DC, it only adds it when you choose "Spell" for the spellcasting ability. Otherwise, choosing any other stat (even the one that is the same as your spellcasting ability) WON'T gain the bonus from the magic item. I think you can change what is your "Spellcasting Ability" in the Attributes & Abilities tab of your character sheet. I haven't tested this yet, though. In fact, I haven't tested this extensively, I just noticed it now while playing. (edited)

So for example: You're a Sorc/Wiz. Your spell tab says "Spellcasting Ability" is Charisma. You have +1 Bloodwell Vial which is supposed to add only to your Sorcerer spells. Setting all your Wizard spells to have "Spellcasting Ability: Intelligence" will prevent the bloodwell vial adding +1 to the attack/dc when you click to send it to Roll20 chat. You could even change those Wizard spells to Charisma, and it won't add it. It will only add the +1 from the vial when you have "Spell" selected as the Spellcasting Ability.
